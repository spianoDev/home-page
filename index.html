My path to development...

To fully understand my story, you need a little background. I am a pianist and a music teacher. Together those two things have meant sporadic employment and multiple year end conversations about budget and programming cuts. I’ve finally had enough.

I started dabbling in coding with freeCodeCamp in March 2018, and went through their courses at a break-neck speed. I spent two chunks of time doing that but ultimately felt I needed some interaction with others to help me with the more complex JavaScript concepts. I also wanted to delve into some back end programming.

Consequently, over the 2019 summer, I discovered Coursera.org and created my own quasi-boot camp by taking a variety of courses learning python, PHP, SQL, and more JavaScript. While this “work at your own pace” was great and I was gobbling up enormous amounts of content, I wasn’t getting any personalized feedback. (Actually there was one course that did offer this through mentors and that was definitely a stand out experience for me!) I also realized that as much as I was learning, it was mostly theory independent from practical application to website building. Sure I can write a function, but how can I make it work with my HTML output?

At this point, I had no way to build a network and I was (am?) very intimidated about the prospect of a technical interview - a requirement if I’m serious about making web development a new career. All these factors and another pink slip from yet another school have pushed me to finally take the plunge into a fully immersive, software engineering bootcamp through General Assembly.

Come November, I will be a GA graduate on a quest for a junior development position.
